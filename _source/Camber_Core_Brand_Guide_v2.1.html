Typography

Fonts, weights, and sizing

Primary Display Typeface: Raleway

Raleway is the brand's primary display typeface, used for the logo wordmark, all headings, and labels. Its elegant, geometric letterforms give the spaced uppercase wordmark and section headings a confident, refined character, and they hold up especially well in the wide-tracked capitals of the logo and section titles. Raleway is free and open-source (Google Fonts): it embeds cleanly on the web and is free to install for Office and print, so the same display type renders across the website, proposals, resumes, and documents. Weights in use: Light (300), Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700), ExtraBold (800).

Body & Long-Form Copy: Questrial

Body copy is set in Questrial, a clean geometric sans that pairs naturally with Raleway while staying highly readable in running text. Questrial ships in a single Regular (400) weight, so emphasis and hierarchy come from size, color, and the Raleway display face rather than heavier cuts of the same family. For dense, multi-page documents such as agreements and statements of work, hold body size at 10.5 to 11pt with generous line spacing (1.5 to 1.6) to keep long passages comfortable to read.

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain a clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "&" character on all sides. The minimum reproduction width for the full wordmark is 120px (digital) or 1.5 inches (print).

Document Standards

Templates and formatting conventions

Letterhead

Header features the wordmark with tagline, a gold divider rule, and a contact bar (email, phone, website). Footer carries the address, company name, and page number separated by a thin gold rule. Standard margins: 1 inch all sides, with top margin at 1.67 inches to accommodate the header.

Corporate Documents (Agreements, SOWs)

Title page with gold accent lines, wordmark, document title, metadata block (date, version, classification), and confidentiality notice. Body pages carry a compact header (wordmark left, document title right) and footer (company + confidential left, website center, page right).

Voice & Tone

How we communicate
Professional & Confident

We speak with authority earned through deep technical expertise. No hedging, no filler: clear, direct statements backed by data.

Clear & Accessible

Technical precision without jargon overload. Government stakeholders and technical teams alike should immediately understand our message.

Precise & Factual

We lead with specifics: numbers, metrics, outcomes. "75+ hours saved per project" not "significant time savings."

Collaborative & Responsive

We partner with clients, not just deliver to them. Our tone reflects a trusted advisor who listens, adapts, and follows through.
